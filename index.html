Project overview

This project digs into consumer lending risk from three angles on the same idea, borrower records, loan level information, and mortgage related consumer complaints. The question I kept coming back to was how borrower characteristics, loan structure, repayment pressure, and complaint patterns actually connect to credit quality and default related outcomes.

I wrote it up as a journal that follows the eleven modules of the course rather than as a single report, so each page covers what I did, what I found, and what it meant from a risk perspective. The point is to show how the project came together, not just where it landed.

Datasets used

German Credit Data

Borrower level credit risk data used for risk classification and feature interpretation.

Lending Club Loans

Loan level data used for borrower segmentation, PCA, regression, and loan outcome analysis.

CFPB Complaints API

Mortgage related complaint data used to connect lending risk to borrower experience.
